Method and sources

Treasury-first data, official evidence, and regime-path inference.

The system is designed to be economically grounded, Treasury-specific, and explicit about what is directly observed versus structurally inferred.

Handbook

Hidden Treasury market layer primer

The visible curve is only the surface. The primer page walks through dealer balance-sheet pressure, cash-futures basis, repo specials, auction digestion, on-the-run richness, hedging flow, and positioning stress in a repeatable desk-style reading format.

Data

Public numeric sources

  • FRED: Treasury curve, real yields, breakevens, funding and macro releases.
  • Treasury Fiscal Data: auctions, upcoming issuance, and debt-management context.
  • Official New York Fed / Treasury channels: communication that changes policy and issuance interpretation.
Evidence

Structured source claims

Official releases and speeches are converted into structured claims by axis, channel, direction, horizon, and linked player. Media references are summarized and cited rather than republished.

Models

Economic, statistical, and ML logic

  • Fixed regime dictionary plus sparse transition graph.
  • Source-decomposed Bayesian posterior updates by horizon.
  • Market-feature channel scores for Treasury, inflation, policy, activity, and shock-player evidence.
  • Softmax action distributions for five-player strategy paths.
  • Daily, weekly, and monthly review of scenario and source accuracy.
Public caution

What the site does not do

This is not an intraday flow model, and it does not claim direct visibility into dealer books or proprietary client data. It uses public proxies and structured evidence to infer which regime path and player response path are becoming more likely.
